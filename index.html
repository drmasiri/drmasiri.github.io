I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Computing and Information Technology at the University of Bisha. My research focuses on security and resilience in cyber-physical and operational-technology environments, with particular emphasis on industrial control systems (ICS) and critical infrastructure.

About Me

I earned my Ph.D. from Cardiff University in 2025, where I was advised by Professor Neetesh Saxena and conducted my doctoral research in the Cyber and Critical Infrastructure Security (CyCIS) Lab.

A central thread of my work examines indicators of compromise (IoCs) in ICS contexts, including how compromise manifests in operational telemetry, how detection signals translate into actionable response, and why many IT-centric indicators degrade under ICS constraints and threat models. Complementary contributions address detection and incident analysis in critical-infrastructure environments.

I contribute to the research community through peer review for journals and conferences in security and privacy, including ACM and IEEE venues such as ACM Transactions on Cyber-Physical Systems, IEEE Transactions on Information Forensics and Security (TIFS), IEEE Security & Privacy, and SmartCom.

Research

My research program is centered on the security and resilience of critical infrastructure.

ICS-specific IoCs

Developing evidence-driven IoC taxonomies and operational guidance aligned with OT realities such as safety constraints and limited patch windows.

Smart-grid Resilience

Integrating co-simulation and observable indicators to support structured detection, triage, and post-incident reasoning in cyber-physical grid settings.

Security Validation

Assessing detection/defense coverage by mapping observable traces and control efficacy to standardized adversary tactics and techniques (MITRE ATT&CK).

Incident Analysis

Examining how detection signals translate into actionable response and investigating post-incident analysis for critical infrastructure.
